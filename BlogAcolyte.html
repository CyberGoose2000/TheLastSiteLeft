At this point why even bother.

December 23, 2024 by Mark

I have a weird relationship with popular culture. What I mean by that is with most pop culture I'm years behind. This includes listening to music, watching television shows, watching films and playing games, there's a good chance I'm years behind. This has nothing to do with whether it's something I'm interested in like the MCU films and shows or something I'm told from a reliable source I'll like. My old house mate knows me well, he knows what I like. Whenever he would tell me to watch something it wouldn’t be because he liked it but because he knew I would like it. He would get furious at how long it would take me to pick up on anything he told me to watch, read, play etc. What I'm trying to say is I'm behind a lot of the time. And I don’t really keep up on pop culture news and social media. Most of my knowledge of current events going on that are not on the front page of the Guardian website are from my friends. So, I watched The Acolyte recently, one of the Disney Plus Star Wars TV shows. I know Star Wars has had a rocky road since Disney bought it. The fans, as a default, are;


  • Angry at something Disney has done to Star Wars (I say something, I really mean they have a list);
  • Hate half or more of the Star Wars media and take this all very very seriously;
  • Are incapable of making a comment about Star Wars that doesnt relate back to liberals and wokeness ruining the planet.

I'm pretty sure it's the same with most fan bases in 2024. Being a fan has become being in constant combat, gate keeping what being a "true fan" is, implying a relationship in which they are personally owed for their fandom and building up any critique into a hyperbole filled politically damning verbal mauling. It's easy to do, discourse has become irate as standard, discussing has been replaced by shouting points at one another never listening for a response, and there seems to be a real issue in being able to differentiate subjective criticism with objective criticism. This is all difficult enough when the points being squawked about are solely within the realm of the fandom but all too often fandoms have had their loves become inescapably combined with the cultural argument around "wokeness" and all that comes with that. Star Wars has had its issues with the "wokeness" argument since the Force Awakens and the question of whether "diversity" is hurting Star Wars and what ulterior motives Disney may have in its casting choices etc seems to be under constant scrutiny.


So, I have an idea of the general sentiment towards Star Wars (and there is a LOT of sentiment) and I knew that the Acolyte had been received poorly but I'm well beyond listening to reviews for this type of thing, so I decided to watch the whole thing. And I did. Now this is my fault, I did not bother to read anything further until I had watched it. One of the things I thought at the end of the show was "you know this could be one of those shows that really hits its stride two or three seasons in" and then I immediately read that the show had been cancelled. I blame myself for doing this, but I needed to know why, and this is where I am eternally struggling. I don't want to care about this stuff, I don't want to get so worked up, but I do care and apparently, I like to torture myself. Now for my second mistake, I immediately assumed that it was a nerd issue, that they had made changes to the Star Wars world that angered a large amount of the fan base. I was so confused. This is pretty much exactly what the old republic type eras had always been portrayed as. Droids, Jedi, light sabres, Sith, witches, blasters... there was nothing in it that hadn't already been established.
